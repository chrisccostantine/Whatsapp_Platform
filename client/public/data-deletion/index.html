User Data Deletion Instructions

Scalora users and people whose information is stored by a Scalora business can request access, correction, or deletion using these instructions.

Last updated: July 15, 2026

1. Disconnect WhatsApp access

  1. Sign in to Scalora as the workspace owner.
  2. Open Settings, then WhatsApp Cloud API.
  3. Select the disconnect button and confirm. Scalora will disable the connection and replace stored WhatsApp access and verification credentials.
  4. You may also remove Scalora's access from Meta Business settings. Information held independently by Meta or WhatsApp must be managed through those services.

2. Request account or workspace deletion

Email scalora.socialmedia.agency@gmail.com from the address associated with your Scalora account using the subject "Scalora data deletion request".

  • Include your full name, Scalora login email, and workspace or business name.
  • State whether you want your profile, connected Meta/WhatsApp account, specific customer information, or the entire workspace deleted.
  • For Meta/WhatsApp requests, include the business display name and connected phone number. Never send passwords, access tokens, verification codes, or payment details.
  • If you are a customer contact, identify the business that contacted you and the phone number or email associated with your record.

3. Verification and completion

We will acknowledge the request and may verify identity, workspace ownership, or authority. After verification, we aim to delete or de-identify eligible information from active systems within 30 days and confirm by email. Encrypted backups may remain until scheduled expiration for up to 90 days. Limited records may be retained for security, fraud prevention, legal compliance, disputes, or financial recordkeeping.

4. What deletion covers

  • Eligible Scalora profile and authentication records.
  • Selected workspace configuration, team records, CRM contacts, conversations, messages, templates, campaigns, files, reports, and commerce records, subject to legal retention duties.
  • Encrypted Meta and WhatsApp credentials and connection identifiers held by Scalora.
  • Operational records that can reasonably be deleted or de-identified without compromising security or legal obligations.

5. Important limitations

Deleting data from Scalora does not delete copies controlled independently by another workspace member, the business that collected the data, message recipients, Meta, WhatsApp, or another third party. Contact those parties separately where necessary.
