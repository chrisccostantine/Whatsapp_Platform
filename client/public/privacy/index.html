Privacy Policy

This policy explains how Scalora collects, uses, shares, protects, and deletes information when businesses use our CRM and WhatsApp integration.

Last updated: July 15, 2026

1. Who we are and scope

Scalora CRM ("Scalora", "we", "us", or "our") provides a multi-tenant customer relationship management platform for businesses. This policy applies to the Scalora website, application, APIs, and integrations, including the WhatsApp Business Platform integration.

A business that creates a Scalora workspace generally controls the customer and communications data uploaded to that workspace. Scalora processes that data to provide the service on the business's instructions. Scalora is responsible for account, security, and service-usage information collected for our own operations.

2. Information we collect

  • Account and workspace information, including names, business names, email addresses, roles, authentication records, settings, and invitations.
  • CRM and commerce information submitted by a workspace, including customer contact details, tags, notes, pipeline records, follow-ups, products, orders, quotations, invoices, campaigns, and consent records.
  • WhatsApp information, including WhatsApp Business Account and phone-number identifiers, business profile details, templates, message content, attachments, delivery/read status, and conversation metadata.
  • Meta integration data needed to operate the connection, including authorization codes and access tokens. Codes are exchanged on the server, tokens are encrypted at rest, and Scalora does not receive a user's Meta password.
  • Technical and security data, including IP address, browser information, timestamps, audit events, API and error logs, session cookies, and locally stored authentication state.
  • Support communications and information a person chooses to send us.

3. How we use information

  • Provide, secure, maintain, troubleshoot, and improve Scalora and its integrations.
  • Authenticate users, enforce workspace roles, prevent cross-tenant access, and maintain audit trails.
  • Send and receive WhatsApp messages at a workspace's direction, synchronize templates and delivery status, and process Meta webhooks.
  • Manage CRM workflows, documents, reporting, notifications, and collaboration.
  • Respond to support, privacy, security, and legal requests; detect abuse; comply with law; and enforce agreements.

4. Legal grounds and business responsibilities

Depending on the context and applicable law, processing is based on performance of a contract, legitimate interests in operating and securing the service, consent, or compliance with legal obligations. Workspace owners are responsible for having a valid legal basis to add contacts, retain their data, and communicate with them.

Businesses using WhatsApp through Scalora must obtain any required messaging consent, honor opt-outs, and follow the WhatsApp Business Messaging Policy and applicable laws.

5. How information is shared

  • With authorized members of the relevant Scalora workspace according to their roles.
  • With Meta and WhatsApp when a workspace connects WhatsApp or directs Scalora to transmit messages, templates, media, or related requests.
  • With infrastructure and service providers that host, store, secure, monitor, or support Scalora under appropriate safeguards.
  • With advisers, authorities, or other parties when reasonably necessary to comply with law, protect rights and safety, investigate abuse, or handle a business transaction.

6. International processing

Scalora, Meta, and service providers may process information outside a user's country. Where required, we use appropriate contractual or legal safeguards. Workspace owners are responsible for additional transfer requirements applying to data they control.

7. Retention and deletion

We retain information while a workspace is active and as needed to provide the service, meet legal obligations, resolve disputes, prevent abuse, and enforce agreements. After a verified deletion request, we delete or de-identify information not legally required to be retained. We aim to complete active-system deletion within 30 days. Residual encrypted backups may persist for up to 90 days before scheduled expiration. Limited security, audit, transaction, or legal records may be retained where necessary.

8. Security

We use safeguards including tenant-scoped authorization, encrypted integration credentials, encrypted network transport, access controls, input validation, audit logging, and restricted production access. No system is completely secure, and users must protect their credentials and devices.

9. Your choices and rights

Depending on applicable law, a person may have rights to access, correct, export, restrict, object to, or delete personal information. Workspace contacts should normally contact the business controlling their data first. Requests may also be sent to Scalora. We may verify identity and authority before acting.

Users can disconnect WhatsApp in Scalora settings. This does not automatically delete information held independently by Meta, WhatsApp, message recipients, or the connected business.

10. Children

Scalora is a business service and is not directed to children. Users must be at least 18 years old or the legal age required to enter a binding business agreement.

11. Changes

We may update this policy to reflect changes to Scalora, providers, or legal requirements. We will post the update here and change the last-updated date.
